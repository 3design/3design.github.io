HIBALL

Incentivising long[er]-term collaboration

Welcome to HIBALL, the new German-Canadian Lab. Our aim is to transform the successful collaboration between McGill (MNI, BIC) and Forschungszentrum Jülich (INM-1, Jülich Supercomputing Centre - JSC) to the next level by reinforcing utilization and co-development of the latest AI and high-performance computing (HPC) technologies for building highly detailed 3D brain models, in close collaboration with CIFAR and MILA in Canada, and HAICU, the Helmholtz AI coordination unit.

The BigBrain, AI and neuro data science

HIBALL will build on the BigBrain Project’s AI-based 3D reconstruction of brain sections [link to BB Data page], neuroanatomical segmentation of cortical layers and cytoarchitectonic parcellations, and develop novel deep learning (DL) methods relying on the latest HPC architectures to analyze Petabyte-scale neuroscience data.

Towards a realistic multi-modal, multi-scale brain model

This will allow to build a microscopic 3D map of functional neuroanatomy at the cellular level that would replace the familiar maps of Brodmann and others from more than 100 years ago and extend it with data from novel modalities, including 3D maps of neurotransmitter-receptor distributions and high-resolution structural connectivity from polarized light imaging (PLI). This model will find application in neuroimaging, AI research, and brain simulation, thus generating novel brain-inspired architectures for reinforcement learning as well as a highly detailed systems-level network model based on The Virtual Brain (TVB [link to TVB]).

Serving a broad international community

HIBALL will streamline BigBrain infrastructure solutions to become highly interoperable with the brain initiatives in Canada and Europe, complementing ongoing work in the European Human Brain Project [link to HBP] and strengthening its infrastructure.

GOALS

Important measures include a new, transcontinental ICT infrastructure resource for

building a dedicated, web-accessible community interaction via a portal to the HIBALL website and platform

hosting and sharing the massive ultra-high resolution, increased parcellation coverage, and diverse imaging modalities for data extensions to the BigBrain dataset

enabling web-based, interactive visualization of extremely large datasets

providing high interoperability of the platform with other brain initiatives in Canada and Europe, directly complementing ongoing work in the European HBP and ensuring long-term impact in the community
